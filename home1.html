About Me

Suzanne Collins began her career in 1991 writing for children's television, contributing to Nickelodeon shows like Clarissa Explains it All and The Mystery Files of Shelby Woo. She also wrote for preschool shows like Little Bear and Oswald. Later, she worked on Clifford's Puppy Days and Wow! Wow! Wubbzy! During her time on Generation O!, she met author James Proimos, who inspired her to try writing children's books.

Collins found inspiration for her first series, The Underland Chronicles, by contemplating the contrast between urban life and the pastoral setting of Alice in Wonderland. The series, starting with Gregor the Overlander, became a New York Times bestseller and was sold in 21 foreign territories. Her more prominent success came with The Hunger Games Trilogy, an international bestseller with over 100 million copies sold worldwide. The first book spent over six years on The New York Times bestseller list and was adapted into a highly successful film series by Lionsgate.

In addition to her fiction work, Collins released an autobiographical picture book, Year of the Jungle, in 2013, addressing her experiences when her father was deployed to Vietnam. Her books, including When Charlie McButton Lost Power, have been translated into multiple languages and sold in various territories. Suzanne Collins has been recognized with numerous awards, including being named to the TIME 100 list and receiving the Authors Guild Award for Distinguished Service to the Literary Community in 2016. The film adaptations of The Hunger Games franchise collectively earned nearly $3 billion worldwide.
